Use our data to power your site or app

We've seen first-hand that one of the biggest barriers to solving the Flint water crisis has been a lack of coordination between relief efforts and resources. This means that on-the-ground organizations don't always have enough information to do their jobs, and new online tools are released every week that rely on limited government data about a small number of official distribution sites. The result is that resources never make it to some of the most under-resourced folks in this community we call home.


We want to equalize access to relief resources for all Flint residents, so we're offering our up-to-date community-gathered data on people-powered distribution sites via API (application programming interface) to anyone who wants to integrate it into their website or app.


We'll post documentation for our API here by February 22nd. Please check back to register for an API key and start developing using community-gathered data.

Home-grown data: Young people from the neighborhood volunteer to unload, cart, and carry tons of water each week using makeshift equipment, a humanitarian spirit of goodwill, and muscle-power made of sheer necessity. We get our data the same way.
