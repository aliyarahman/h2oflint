About this site

What is FlintH2O

“A first-world solution to a third-world situation.”
We are a Flint-based, community-led, media-centered start-up. The concept for this digital platform was made by “subterranean” community and grassroots leaders and Flint residents who have been physically serving people affected by the crisis for the last several months through various community-operated distribution hubs. Our aim is to leverage technology to change the human condition, to make sure all residents have access to receive the resources, information, and services they need, want, and deserve. Flint H2O was launched on February 24, 2016.

“An essential part of any sensible, sustainable, long-term plan for water distribution, crowd-sourced intelligence, and economic development.”
Months into the crisis, the City of Flint, Michigan continues in a state of emergency over water. To a significant segment of our population, there also continues to be a lack of coordination, communication, and centralization of pertinent Water Crisis information being readily, reliably, available to the people of Flint. We hope this digital online platform will help us all get and give the help that is needed.


Who built this web app?

In response to the ongoing water crisis in the city, Darnell Ishmel, a Flint resident, has led the development of an innovative, web-based, digital online platform to effectively coordinate fresh water intake and distribution efforts.

Who is this site for?

FlintH2o is for EVERYONE inside and outside of Flint, Michigan - including individuals, grassroots and Faith-Based organizations, non-profit organizations, and for profit businesses. Governmental entities and educational institutions are also invited to use this site. Most especially, this site was designed to serve those who Darnell Ishmel refers to as the City’s ‘subterranean populations’ – those of us who exist well below the grassroots. We, down here, need it the most. We are the ones most marginalized, most neglected, and most affected by this crisis. Technology should help make us all equal. It is 2016. There should be no digital divide.
